Mercedes F1 Infringement Summary - 2024
==================================================

 Lewis Hamilton is fined €5,000 for dangerous release of Car 44 . Lewis Hamilton crossed the white line at pit entry when entering the pits . The manoeuvre was not considered dangerous in any way, therefore the least severe penalty available is imposed . The following Power Unit elements have been replaced without approval of the FIA Technical Delegate during Parc Ferme . George Russell failed to slow under yellow flags after running into the runoff at Turn 2 . This was a similar case to the decision of this panel of Stewards regarding Charles Leclerc with some important differences .  Car 63 (796.5 kg) was below the minimum weight required (798.0 kg) Weight of Car 63 is disqualified from the Race classification . Car 44 (Lewis Hamilton) unnecessarily impeded Car 11 in turn 9 . Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS F1 Team is fined €5,000. The Stewards recommend that this matter be clarified in the next Drivers’ meeting and note that these penalties should not represent a binding precedent . Lewis Hamilton is fined for impeding Car 11 (Sergio Perez) and Sergio Perez for not slowing down more .  Lewis Hamilton received a drive through penalty for speeding in the pit lane during a sprint session race . The Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS F1 Team is fined €15,000 for failing to warn their driver of the fact that Car 44 was arriving on a fast lap . Lewis Hamilton was also fined for unnecessarily impeding Car 2 in Turn 11 at 20:16. The FIA transponder indicated a false start. The Stewards reviewed positioning/marshalling system data, video, timing and in-car video evidence . The driver of Car 63 failed to maintain a 10 car-length distance behind the Safety Car .  The driver left the grid although the aborted start signal was given . The FIA accepted that given this short notice it was extremely difficult if not impossible for the teams to follow the procedure prescribed in the technical directive . Tyre pressures were adjusted (pressure released) with wheels already fitted to the car in contravention to TD003. The FIA Technical Delegate stipulated that the tyre pressures while having been adjusted by the team was within the allowed parameters . The decision should not be considered as a precedent for any similar breach in the future as the circumstances are considered unique .  The team failed to cover and continued to work on the car while under Parc Ferme conditions . Normally a breach of this nature, within a competitive session would carry a sporting penalty but it is not appropriate in this case . However, this decision should not be considered as a precedent for any similar breach in the future as the circumstances are considered unique . Forcing car 77 off the track at turn 12 was not deliberate, and the driver of Car 63 was in control of the car at all times . A 5 second penalty is imposed instead of the standard 10 second penalty .
